Two extractors, not one
Double extraction, restored

Cochrane and JBI both require double data extraction — two independent reviewers per cell. Most AI extraction tools quietly skip this and hand you a single-model output. We do not. E1 and E2 extract independently, then a reconciler returns exactly one verdict per cell: AGREE_E1, AGREE_E2, BOTH_WRONG, AMBIGUOUS, or MISSING_SOURCE. AGREE and BOTH_WRONG resolve automatically. Only AMBIGUOUS and MISSING_SOURCE reach a human.

Human-in-Loop

Judgment, not data entry
Where humans actually add value

~80% of extraction work is mechanical: transcribing numbers, matching intervention names, chasing supplements. AI does this reliably. The human reviewer only touches cells the loop flags AMBIGUOUS or MISSING_SOURCE — typically a small fraction of extractions. That is judgment: clinical reasoning, methodological calls, edge cases. Which is exactly what a domain expert should be spending their time on.

[ 05 ] Methods

Two independent extractors.
Reconcile every cell.

The published literature reports ~91% accuracy for single-model LLM extraction of clinical trial data (Gartlehner et al. 2025) [1]. Stellar Evidence runs two independent extractors on every paper, reconciles them cell-by-cell against a numeric tolerance, and routes only genuinely disputed values to a human reviewer. The target on verified outputs after the loop is 99%.

01

Two independent extractors

Two extractors (typically two different LLMs, or the same model with different seeds) work the same paper independently. Each writes a full extraction to its own Excel workbook using the same trial-and-arm-keyed row template. Neither can see the other's answers.

02

Confidence tags on every cell

Every extracted value carries a provenance tag: [DS] directly stated, [INF] inferred, [CALC] calculated, [SUP] from supplement, [FIG] read from figure, and [PDF-p.N: "quote"] with the exact source quote.

03

Explicit missing-value codes

Missingness has a code, not a blank: -99 not reported, -88 unreadable (scanned or figure-only), -77 arm absent from the trial. The reconciler treats each code differently.

04

Cell-by-cell reconciliation with tolerance

A separate reconciler agent — never one of the extractors — compares the two workbooks cell by cell. Numeric values agree if they fall within max(1% relative, 0.5 absolute). Rounding artefacts pass; real disagreements do not.

05

Five verdicts per disagreement

When extractors disagree, the reconciler consults the source PDF and returns exactly one verdict: AGREE_E1, AGREE_E2, BOTH_WRONG, AMBIGUOUS, or MISSING_SOURCE. AGREE and BOTH_WRONG are resolved automatically. Only AMBIGUOUS and MISSING_SOURCE reach a human.

06

Human review only where needed

Human reviewers see side-by-side extractions, the reconciler's rationale, and the exact [PDF-p.N: "quote"] the models cited. In our 67-paper study only a small fraction of cells reached this step. Resolved cells are marked in the workbook so the audit trail is intact.

07

Annotated source PDFs

After reconciliation, an annotator opens every PDF and highlights the exact quote each cited value came from — yellow highlight, page-by-page, saved to an annotated_sources/ folder. Cochrane, JBI, and TRIPOD-LLM require this level of provenance; peer reviewers get to see exactly where every number came from.

08

Full audit trail out

Deliverables per review: two extractor workbooks, the reconciled master workbook, a JSON reconciliation log (every cell + verdict + rationale), and the annotated-source PDFs. TRIPOD-LLM reporting boilerplate for the methods section is generated automatically.

[ 06 ] Implementation Study

67 papers.
Same dataset. Blinded review.

We ran a head-to-head internal validation comparing extraction by an experienced human research team against the same team assisted by Stellar Evidence's two-extractor loop. Ground truth was the source paper itself, adjudicated by a blinded third reviewer.

Study design

Corpus: 67 published papers, same dataset for both arms.

Data type: categorical clinical trial data — endpoints, arms, risk-of-bias domains, effect estimates.

Arm 1 (Human-only): experienced research team extracting with standard double-extraction workflow.

Arm 2 (Human+AI): same team, same corpus, using Stellar Evidence's two-extractor + reconciler + human-adjudication loop.

Ground truth: the source paper. A blinded third reviewer adjudicated every disagreement between an extraction and the source.

[ 09 ] Why this matters

Faster evidence.
Better care.

Systematic reviews are the gold standard of clinical evidence — the basis for treatment guidelines, drug approvals, and healthcare policy. They are also slow. Compressing that timeline honestly, without sacrificing rigor, has consequences for two groups.

For patients

Evidence that arrives before the diagnosis, not after it.

A systematic review that takes 18 months to finish is a review that isn't shaping today's guidelines. New therapies, new subgroup findings, and safety signals sit inside published trials for months before they reach clinical practice. When the synthesis lag shrinks from months to days, the evidence that reaches a clinician's decision reflects what the field actually knows — not what it knew a year and a half ago.

For researchers

Judgement, not data entry.

Roughly 80% of a systematic review is spent on mechanical work: transcribing tables, coding risk-of-bias domains, cross-checking numbers. That's not where a clinician-scientist's expertise lives. The interpretation, the framing, the judgement about what the evidence actually implies for practice — those cannot and should not be automated. When the tool takes the transcription and the reviewer keeps the interpretation, both parts of the review improve.

For rigor

A second reader on every cell.

Cochrane recommends double extraction; most published reviews don't do it, because two humans working the same 63 papers takes twice as long. A two-extractor AI loop with a human reconciler restores that Cochrane standard — every cell is read by two extractors, disagreements are surfaced with the exact PDF quote, and a human decides the tie. In the 67-paper study, this configuration cut extraction errors by 60% against a human-only baseline.

Evidence-based medicine is only as fast, as accurate, and as auditable as the tooling underneath it. Stellar Evidence is a bet that all three can move at the same time.
